Reality is not singular in motion. It splits. At every moment, systems encounter branching- different inputs, different states, different interpretations.

## Collapse

A system without branching forces all change into a single path. Every decision overwrites the previous state. Continuity becomes fragile, because alternative realities are lost. There's only one truth, one direction, one context.

## Separation

We usually tackle branches as separate worlds- untouching, isolated, oblivious to one another. Each branch flows in another direction- holding its own context, its own lifetime- yet detached from the reality that birthed it. A branch that breaks communication and it endears forgetting.

## Holding hands

A branch is a continuation of the same universe- a universe that holds onto its origin. Branches don't separate, they [[Unity|unify]]. They're not isolated timelines as they are parts of the same continuity graph.
