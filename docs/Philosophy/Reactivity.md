Software spends much of its life rediscovering reality. Files re-read, processes polled, states refreshed. Change exists, yet systems rarely treat it as information.

Nobody waits- everyone asks "has this changed yet?". 

The result is a system constantly interrogating itself for changes that have already happened.

## Patience

In a reactive system, change is not discovered through interrogation. It is propagated as part of the system's awareness. Components do not continuously ask: "has this changed yet?", instead, change becomes part of the system's language. When reality changes, those who depend on it are informed.  

Reaction ceases to be a search for truth to become a response to it.

## Promise of [[Continuity]]

A truly continuous system guarantees reactivity at it's core. 

Change must be [[Communication#Understanding|understood]] before it can be preserved. A system cannot continue through realities it never became aware of- Every mutation, creation, replacement, or removal becomes part of the continuity it seeks to maintain.
